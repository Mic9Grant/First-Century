The First Century CE

A Transformative Era in Human History

c. 4 BCE - 30/33 CE
Life and Crucifixion of Jesus Christ
The birth, ministry, and crucifixion of Jesus of Nazareth established the foundation of Christianity.
27 BCE - 14 CE
Establishment of the Roman Empire
Augustus became the first Roman emperor, ending the Republic and initiating the Pax Romana.
c. 33-67 CE
Ministry of Paul the Apostle
Paul's missionary journeys spread Christianity throughout the Roman Empire.
43 CE
Roman Conquest of Britain
Emperor Claudius ordered the invasion of Britain, beginning nearly four centuries of Roman rule.
64 CE
Great Fire of Rome
A devastating fire destroyed much of Rome under Emperor Nero, leading to the first major Christian persecution.
69 CE
Year of the Four Emperors
Following Nero's suicide, civil war erupted as four claimants fought for the imperial throne.
70 CE
Destruction of Jerusalem
Roman forces destroyed Jerusalem and the Second Temple, beginning the Jewish diaspora.
79 CE
Mount Vesuvius Eruption
The volcanic eruption destroyed Pompeii and Herculaneum, preserving them for posterity.
